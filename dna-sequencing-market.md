<p><p>The DNA sequencing market is categorized into three main types: First Generation, Second Generation, and Third Generation sequencing. First Generation sequencing, primarily Sanger sequencing, offers high accuracy but low throughput. Second Generation, or next-generation sequencing (NGS), significantly increases throughput and cost-effectiveness, enabling high-volume sequencing. Third Generation sequencing provides real-time analysis and longer read lengths, enhancing accuracy and completeness of genomes. Each generation builds on the previous one, advancing the capabilities and applications of genomic research and biotechnology.</p></p>
