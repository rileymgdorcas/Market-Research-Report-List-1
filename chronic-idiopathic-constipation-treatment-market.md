<p><p>The treatment market for chronic idiopathic constipation includes various diagnostic types. Blood tests assess for underlying conditions affecting bowel functions. Sigmoidoscopy and colonoscopy allow direct visualization of the colon to identify abnormalities. The balloon expulsion test evaluates rectal function by measuring the ability to expel a balloon. Anorectal manometry assesses the function of the rectum and anal sphincter. A colonic transit study measures how quickly food moves through the colon, while defecography visualizes the process of defecation. Other methods may include imaging techniques or clinical evaluations.</p></p>
