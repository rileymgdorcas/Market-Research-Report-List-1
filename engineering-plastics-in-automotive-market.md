<p><p>Engineering plastics in the automotive market are categorized into two main types: thermoplastics and thermosetting plastics. Thermoplastics, such as polycarbonate and nylon, are versatile, easily molded, and can be reshaped upon heating, making them ideal for lightweight components and ease of manufacturing. In contrast, thermosetting plastics, like epoxy and phenolic resin, harden permanently after curing, providing exceptional durability and heat resistance, crucial for high-performance applications. Both types contribute significantly to enhancing vehicle efficiency and performance while reducing weight.</p></p>
