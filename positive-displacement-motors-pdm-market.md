<p><p>Positive Displacement Motors (PDM) operate by trapping a fixed volume of fluid and displacing it to generate motion. In the market, they are categorized into rotor and stator types. Rotor-type PDMs, such as gear and vane motors, utilize rotating elements to achieve motion, while stator-type designs, like diaphragm motors, rely on fixed elements to contain and direct fluid flow. Both types are essential in various applications, including hydraulic systems and industrial machinery, offering efficiency and reliability for fluid-driven processes.</p></p>
