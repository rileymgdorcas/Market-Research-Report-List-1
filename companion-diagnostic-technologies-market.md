<p><p>Companion diagnostic technologies encompass tools that enhance patient-specific treatment efficacy, particularly in oncology. The two main market types are immunohistochemistry (IHC) and molecular diagnostics. IHC utilizes antibody-based techniques to identify specific proteins in tissue samples, guiding therapy decisions based on the presence or absence of biomarkers. Molecular diagnostics involve analyzing genetic material—DNA or RNA—to detect mutations or alterations, informing targeted therapies. Together, these technologies play a critical role in personalized medicine by improving treatment outcomes and optimizing therapeutic strategies.</p></p>
