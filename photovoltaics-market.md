<p><p>The photovoltaics market is divided into two main types: organic and inorganic materials. Organic photovoltaics utilize carbon-based compounds to convert sunlight into electricity, offering flexibility and potential for low-cost production but generally lower efficiency and stability. In contrast, inorganic photovoltaics, predominantly based on silicon, are more widely used due to their higher efficiency, robustness, and established manufacturing processes. Both markets are evolving, with ongoing research aimed at improving performance and reducing costs to meet growing energy demands.</p></p>
