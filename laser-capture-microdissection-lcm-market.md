<p><p>Laser Capture Microdissection (LCM) is a technique used to isolate specific cells or tissue components from heterogeneous samples using a laser. The market is divided into two main types: Infrared Laser and UV Laser. Infrared Laser LCM utilizes low-energy lasers to cut and capture samples without damaging surrounding tissue, making it ideal for live cell studies. In contrast, UV Laser LCM employs high-energy lasers for precise dissection, often used in applications requiring minimal thermal effect, such as genomic and proteomic analyses.</p></p>
