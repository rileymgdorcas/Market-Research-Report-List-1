<p><p>RFID electronic ear tags operate across three primary market types based on frequency: Low Frequency (LF), High Frequency (HF), and Ultra High Frequency (UHF). LF tags, typically operating between 125 kHz to 134 kHz, are used for applications requiring close-range reading and are less affected by environmental factors. HF tags, ranging from 13.56 MHz, offer moderate read range and data transfer speeds, suitable for tracking livestock. UHF tags, from 300 MHz to 960 MHz, enable longer read distances and faster data transmission, making them ideal for large-scale operations and inventory management.</p></p>
