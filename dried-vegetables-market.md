<p><p>The dried vegetables market encompasses various types, primarily freeze-dried and air-dried vegetables. Freeze-dried vegetables undergo a process that removes moisture while preserving flavor, color, and nutrients, making them lightweight and shelf-stable, ideal for long-term storage. In contrast, air-dried vegetables are dehydrated using warm air, resulting in a different texture and taste. Both types cater to distinct consumer needs, with freeze-dried options favored for convenient meal preparation and air-dried varieties often used in soups, snacks, and gourmet dishes.</p></p>
