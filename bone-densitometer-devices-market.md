<p><p>The bone densitometer devices market is segmented into several types. Dual Energy X-Ray Absorptiometry (DXA) is the most widely used, providing precise measurements of bone density. Peripheral Dual Energy X-Ray Absorptiometry (pDXA) measures bone density at peripheral sites, such as the wrist or heel, offering portability and lower costs. Quantitative Ultrasound (QUS) uses sound waves to assess bone quality, requiring no radiation. Other methods may include MRI or CT imaging techniques, which also contribute to evaluating bone health.</p></p>
