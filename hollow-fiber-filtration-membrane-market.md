<p><p>Hollow fiber filtration membranes are categorized into two primary types: microfiltration and ultrafiltration. Microfiltration utilizes larger pore sizes to remove particles, bacteria, and suspended solids, making it suitable for preliminary water treatment and beer production. Ultrafiltration, with smaller pore sizes, effectively separates viruses, proteins, and colloids, often used in water purification, food processing, and pharmaceuticals. Both technologies offer efficient filtration solutions, ensuring high-quality water and product clarity across various industrial applications.</p></p>
