<p><p>Isostatic pressing is a technique used to uniformly apply pressure to materials, with two primary market types: wet bag pressing and dry bag pressing. Wet bag pressing utilizes a liquid medium to create pressure, ideal for complex shapes and materials sensitive to heat. In contrast, dry bag pressing employs air or gas, providing versatility for various materials while minimizing contamination risks. Both methods serve industries such as aerospace, automotive, and medical, enabling the production of high-density components with enhanced mechanical properties.</p></p>
