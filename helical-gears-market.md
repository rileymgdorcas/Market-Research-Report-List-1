<p><p>Helical gears are categorized based on the orientation of their helicoid angles into left-hand and right-hand types. Left-hand helical gears have teeth that spiral in a counterclockwise direction when viewed from one end, while right-hand gears spiral clockwise. The choice between these gears affects the direction of rotation and load transmission in machinery. Understanding these orientations is crucial for engineers to ensure proper gear meshing and efficient operation in various mechanical applications, influencing design choices and performance.</p></p>
